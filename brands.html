Camera & Lens Brands

The photography world is filled with diverse people with different interests. Different camera brands cater best to certain people, and it is important to know about what camera brands accomplish what best! So what camera brands would work for you? And what lenses go best for your budget, and needs?

Sony Canon Nikon Olympus Leica
Sony is a great brand for hybrids. They offer good video cameras that still offer great photo support, and vice versa. Along with this, they offer some of the best optical quality you can get! Canon is the most popular and one of the best photography options. They offer a range of cameras for all budgets, loads of super zoom lenses, and some great optical quality! Nikon was my starting place, they're great for their quick performance, cheaper lenses and great photo quality. They excel for wildlife photographers! Olympus is a lesser used, but still very capable brand. Their cameras offer great stabilisation, autofocus, and are great against the weather, making them great for adventurers! Leica jumps up to the most expensive end. Their cameras are sought after and great for street photographers. Their cameras are expensive due to the quality of their buid, and they produce less due to the smaller nature of their company.
Hasselblad Panasonic Fujifilm Pentax Kodak
If you own a hasselblad, it's the equivalent to owning a porsche amongst civics. Hasselblad cameras are handmade, their built in filter allows for very natural, true colour, and amazing quality! Panasonic cameras (known as Lumix), and they offer loads of point and shoot options, along with SLR options. They have great sensors, and work well for vloggers and photographers. Fujifilm is well known for their polaroid cameras, offering point and shoot photo printers that many love. Their cameras are great for hobbyists, and they offer loads of vintage, or unique fun features. A professional photographer may prefer other options over Fujifilm. Pentax, an also lesser used brand, is great for budget options, providing good quality and a lower price. They have great lenses for bokeh, and they offer high quality for a lower price, but they do sacrifice in some other places. Last but not least, Kodak, a brand that has been creating cameras for ages, focusing on film cameras and development, they paved the way for film cameras in the 70s and 80s.
