ISO

↩ Previous Page
→ Next Page

ISO is a camera setting that lets you adjust how sensitive your cameras is to light! Sort of like an exposure slider, a high ISO, such as 2000 will make light super visible, which is super useful for low light situations. The common standard for ISO is 100, it is a low, natural setting that ensures your photo wont be overexposed. ISO 100 is best for daylight, and is what most photos are shot with. See below for a list of ISO scenarios!

  • ISO 100: 100 ISO is best for daylight, it minimises grain, and ensures your photo will look natural and crisp!
  • ISO 400: 400 ISO is best for shade, it brings out some light that the camera may not see like how your eyes see it, and it will catch the heavily shadowed spaces. This also may be good for sunset where light isn't as abundant, or strong!
  • ISO 1000: 1000 ISO is best for indoors without light, or outdoors at sunset / dusk. This will introduce a bit of grain into your photos, but it will capture light that you can't see, bringing out objects that may be hard for you to see.
  • ISO >1000: Anything greater than 1000 is best for night skies, dark landscapes, and any situation where your other settings are creating a dark scene. You can use ISO 1000 or more in broad daylight if your shutter is too fast and your aperture is high, it is all dependant on scenario!
